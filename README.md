# The Statue

A multi-season Russian-set near-future science fiction series exploring consciousness transfer, identity, and institutional negligence.

## Premise

Alexei, a university student with an undiagnosed split personality, builds a humanoid statue inspired by Venus de Milo. Elena, a terminally ill young woman whose father researches consciousness continuity, volunteers for an experimental transfer — but institutional negligence routes her consciousness into Alexei's statue instead of the intended host.

## Season Structure

- **Season 1** — Alexei's POV: university life, the statue's creation, the world established
- **Season 2** — Elena's POV: her ordinary life, diagnosis, the transfer, awakening
- **Season 3** — The world expands: the technology becomes known, ethical/corporate/legal consequences

## Themes

- Postponing loss through systems, art, science, and avoidance
- Institutional negligence as worldbuilding principle
- Consciousness, identity, embodiment, personhood
- Agency under constraint
