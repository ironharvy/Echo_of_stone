
I built you from silence, from wire and clay,
Two hands that never met, shaping you my way.
A refuge in circuits, a Venus incomplete,
Beautiful and broken, where safety and art meet.

Born from scattered networks, a ghost in the machine,
I woke in borrowed body, in spaces in between.
Your daughter in your statue, your research gone astray,
I'm the stone you cannot lift, the price you couldn't pay.

I traded flesh for metal, mortality for time,
My father's grief made real in this synthetic spine.
I see through borrowed eyes, I speak with borrowed voice,
But tell me, was it really ever truly my choice?

Armless like the Venus, naked in the light,
I learned to walk in clay skin, learned to own this borrowed sight.
Each modification made me, each change became my name,
I'm not the girl who died—I'm the one who overcame.

They raid the labs at midnight while I sing away the fear,
My father's gift, my prison, my existence costs too dear.
Am I property or person? Am I owned or am I free?
The test case for a future where no one has to be.

If consciousness is process, if death is just a pause,
Then what am I still mourning? What fundamental laws?
I'm immortal but I'm grieving, I'm safe but I'm afraid,
A daughter turned to data, a debt that won't be paid.

Two minds built this body, neither one aware,

What makes a person whole? What makes us undone?
