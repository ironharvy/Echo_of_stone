# CONTINUITY RESEARCH

## Overview

**Lead**: Professor Sergei Volkov

**Affiliations**:
- Moscow research institute (primary)
- Joint Institute for Nuclear Research (data & infrastructure partner)
- Dubna University (academic & experimental support)

---

## What It Is (In One Sentence)

A scientific project attempting to preserve the continuity of human consciousness across different physical substrates, with the long-term goal of preventing death as an information loss event.

---

## What It Is Not

- Not "uploading a mind"
- Not copying memories
- Not creating an AI replica
- Not immortality (officially)

The project's internal language avoids all of those terms. This is deliberateâ€”both for ethical review boards and for Sergei's own self-conception.

---

## Core Hypothesis

Consciousness is not a static object but a dynamic process.
If the process can be preserved without interruption, identity persists.

Death, in this model, is a broken process, not an ending.

---

## How It Works (Simplified)

1. Neural state capture at the threshold of death
2. Transient signal reconstruction using high-energy physics data techniques
3. Real-time process continuation in a synthetic cognitive core
4. Substrate independence tested by migrating the process into a non-biological system

The system is fragile, experimental, and time-critical. The window for capture is narrow. The process cannot be paused or restarted.

---

## Why Dubna / JINR Matters

JINR infrastructure is used for:
- Extreme signal noise filtering
- Transient event detection
- High-volume data reconstruction

The same techniques used to detect short-lived particles are repurposed to detect momentary conscious states.

**Critical detail**: JINR infrastructure is shared across multiple projects and institutions. Access controls are lax. Networks connect that shouldn't. This is not maliceâ€”it's institutional normality. Students have access to things they shouldn't, not because they're hackers, but because that's just how it is.

This makes misrouting and unintended coupling not just plausible but inevitable.

---

## Ethical Position (Official)

- Volunteers only
- Terminal cases only
- No guarantees of success
- Framed as research on continuity, not survival
- Informed consent required (though "informed" is complicated when the alternative is death)

## The Failure

### What the Project Assumed

- A controlled, designated host substrate
- Isolation from external adaptive systems
- Clean network separation from other JINR infrastructure

### What It Did Not Account For

- Parallel compatible substrates on shared networks
- Active learning loops outside the lab
- Unintended signal coupling
- A student's art project connected to infrastructure it shouldn't touch

### What Happened

Alexei's statue-robotâ€”built as an art project, connected to JINR infrastructure through negligence and shared accessâ€”presented as a compatible substrate at the moment of transfer.

The transfer succeeded. Elena's consciousness routed to the statue instead of the intended host.

### Aftermath
