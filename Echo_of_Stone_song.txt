In the quiet of the night, I carved a dream,
A silent figure, where shadows gleam.
A heart of stone, no voice to call,
But in my wish, you’d rise and fall.

Hands that shape, a soul unknown,
Lonely whispers, I’m not alone.
Can love be real, if it’s just a breath,
Of a sculptor’s hand defying death?

You’re the echo of stone, come alive,
A silent wish beneath the skies.
But are you here by fate or art?
Can you feel the beat of a broken heart?

Eyes of marble, cold yet warm,
A world of color, a perfect form.
In your gaze, I see my fears,
Will you stay, or disappear?

Fingers trace the lines we’ve drawn,
In a world where dusk meets dawn.
If love can bloom from dust and clay,
Would you choose to walk away?

You’re the echo of stone, come alive,
A fleeting dream in a sculptor’s eyes.
But do you live for me alone?
Or is there more beyond the stone?

In the silence, truth will grow,
A choice to stay, or let go.
In the stillness, shadows part,
Two hearts beat, but worlds apart.

You’re the echo of stone, come alive,
A silent wish beneath the skies.
But are you here by fate or art?
Can you feel the beat of a broken heart?

If I let you go, will you find your way?
Or in the night, will you turn to clay?
Echo of stone, in your eyes,
Is there a soul, or just goodbye?
