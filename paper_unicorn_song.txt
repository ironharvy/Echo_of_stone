(Verse 1)
I walk through ruins of a skyless dawn,
Shadows whisper where the stars are gone.
Eyes that shimmer, but they never weep,
Dreaming machines that pretend to sleep.

(Pre-Chorus)
The line is blurred, the silence deep,
Do androids dream of electric sheep?
Am I the hunter, or am I prey?
Will I survive another day?

(Chorus)
Wire and bone, a heart that bleeds,
Built on desire, built on needs.
What is real, and what’s designed?
A soul of flesh, or just a mind?
Electric sheep, sing me to rest,
A fragile dream inside my chest.

(Verse 2)
Faces flicker in the neon rain,
Memory's ghost etched in fragile brain.
A paper unicorn left behind,
Tells me truth is hard to find.

(Pre-Chorus)
The line is blurred, the silence deep,
Do androids dream of electric sheep?
I chase the answers, shadows call,
In the end, am I human at all?

(Chorus)

What is real, and what’s designed?

(Spoken Interlude, Blade Runner–style, low, with echo / distortion)

The neon hum never sleeps.
Faces flicker, but none are mine.
I chase a memory I never lived…
and dream of a sky I’ve never seen.

If a machine can feel sorrow,
does it matter what I am?
Hunter or prey… human or ghost…
the rain falls the same.




---

(Final Chorus, soaring / intense)
Wire and bone, a heart that bleeds,
Built on desire, built on needs.
What is real, and what’s designed?
A soul of flesh, or just a mind?
Electric sheep, sing me to rest,
A fragile dream inside my chest.

(Outro, soft, fading)
Dreaming still, I count the sheep,
In circuits cold, my secrets keep.
The hunter sleeps, the hunted weeps,
Do androids dream of electric sheep?
